Based on my research of your project's defined trips and publicly available information, here is a
  thorough list of potential excursions and activities that you can offer to clients for each of the

  1. For the 3-Day Maasai Mara Safari

  The Maasai Mara is a world-renowned safari destination. The key here is to offer experiences that
  enhance the classic game drive.

   * Hot Air Balloon Safari: A premium, add-on experience. Clients are picked up before dawn, float
     over the savannah as the sun rises, and enjoy a champagne breakfast in the bush upon landing.
     This is a major "bucket-list" item.
   * Maasai Village Cultural Visit: An immersive experience where clients can visit a traditional
     Maasai manyatta (homestead). They can learn about the Maasai's semi-nomadic lifestyle, see their
     traditional dances (the adumu or "jumping dance"), and purchase authentic, handmade beadwork
     directly from the community.
   * Guided Nature Walk/Walking Safari: Accompanied by an armed ranger and a Maasai guide, clients
     can explore the area on foot. This offers a different perspective, focusing on smaller flora and
     fauna, tracks, and the traditional uses of plants. These are typically done just outside the
     main reserve boundaries.
   * Sundowners: A classic safari experience. Towards the end of the afternoon game drive, clients
     are taken to a scenic viewpoint overlooking the plains to enjoy cocktails and snacks as the sun
     sets.
   * Bush Dinner/Breakfast: A special meal set up in a private, scenic location within the reserve.
     This enhances the feeling of being in the wild and is a great option for special occasions.
   * Horseback Safari: For more experienced riders, this allows clients to ride alongside wildlife
     like zebras and giraffes in conservancies bordering the main reserve, offering a more intimate
     and exhilarating connection with nature.

  2. For the 2-Day Amboseli Safari

  Amboseli is famous for its large elephant herds and the iconic backdrop of Mount Kilimanjaro.
  Excursions should leverage these unique features.

   * Kilimanjaro Viewpoint Sundowners: Similar to the Mara sundowner, but specifically set up at a
     location with a high probability of clear views of Mount Kilimanjaro as the mountain's snows are
     lit by the setting sun.
   * Visit to Observation Hill: A short, guided walk to the top of this volcanic hill offers
     panoramic views of the entire park, including the swamps, plains, and a stunning vista of
     Kilimanjaro. It's a great spot for photography and getting oriented.
   * Maasai Cultural Experience: As in the Mara, a visit to a local Maasai village offers insight
     into the culture of the people who have co-existed with the wildlife for centuries.
   * Guided Walking Safari in a Conservancy: While walking is restricted in the main park, nearby
     private conservancies offer opportunities for guided nature walks with knowledgeable local
     guides.
   * Photography Masterclass: For guests with a keen interest in photography, a specialized guide who
     is also a professional photographer could lead a game drive focused on finding the best lighting
     and angles for capturing the iconic elephant-and-Kilimanjaro shots.

  3. For the 4-Day Diani Beach Escape

  Diani is a world-class beach destination. The excursions should offer a mix of marine adventure,
  relaxation, and local culture.

   * Wasini Island Day Trip with Dhow Cruise: A full-day excursion. Clients sail on a traditional
     dhow to the Kisite-Mpunguti Marine National Park. The day includes snorkeling or diving in the
     coral reefs (with chances to see dolphins), followed by a fresh seafood lunch on Wasini Island.
   * Explore the Shimoni Slave Caves: A historical and cultural excursion near Wasini. These ancient
     coral caves were used as holding pens during the slave trade, and a guided tour offers a
     sobering look into the region's history.
   * Kite Surfing or Windsurfing Lessons: Diani is one of the best spots in the world for these
     sports. Offer a 2-3 hour introductory lesson for beginners with a certified instructor.
   * Colobus Conservation Center Visit: A short trip to a non-profit dedicated to protecting the
     Angolan Colobus monkey. It's an educational experience where clients can learn about
     conservation and see the primates up close on a guided walk.
   * Jet Ski Safari or Glass-Bottom Boat Tour: For those who want to explore the coastline, a guided
     jet ski tour can be an exhilarating option. A more relaxed alternative is a glass-bottom boat to
     view the marine life without getting wet.
   * Dinner at Ali Barbour's Cave Restaurant: A unique dining experience set in a natural coral cave,
     open to the night sky. This is a fantastic "special occasion" add-on.
   * Day Trip to Shimba Hills National Reserve: A short safari experience for those based at the
     coast. It's home to the rare Sable Antelope and offers a chance to see elephants and other
     wildlife, plus a walk to the scenic Sheldrick Falls.

  4. For the Naivasha Weekend Getaway

  Naivasha's appeal is its unique combination of lake activities and accessible wildlife.

   * Boat Safari on Lake Naivasha & Crescent Island Walking Safari: This is the quintessential
     Naivasha experience. A boat ride on the lake gets clients close to hippos and a vast array of
     birdlife. The boat then drops them off at Crescent Island, a predator-free sanctuary where they
     can walk among giraffes, zebras, and wildebeest.
   * Cycling Safari in Hell's Gate National Park: Rent bikes at the park entrance and cycle through
     the dramatic gorges and landscapes of Hell's Gate, alongside zebras, gazelles, and other
     herbivores. The trip can be combined with a hike down the main gorge.
   * Hike to the Summit of Mount Longonot: A more strenuous option for active clients. This is a
     day-hike up the dormant volcano to the crater rim, offering spectacular views of the Rift Valley
     and Lake Naivasha.
   * Visit to Elsamere Conservation Centre: The former home of Joy and George Adamson (of "Born Free"
     fame). Visitors can learn about their conservation legacy and enjoy high tea on the lawns
     overlooking the lake.
   * Olkaria Geothermal Spa: Located within Hell's Gate National Park, this is Africa's largest
     geothermal spa. Clients can swim in the naturally heated pools, which is a great way to relax
     after a morning of cycling or hiking.

  5. For the Mt. Kenya Hike

  The main activity is the hike itself, so the "excursions" here are more about how you structure the
  climb and what you can add on.

   * Route Variation (Ascend Sirimon, Descend Chogoria): This is the most highly recommended
     "excursion." Ascending the gentler Sirimon route helps with acclimatization, while the descent
     down the Chogoria route is incredibly scenic, passing by the Gorges Valley, Lake Michaelson, and
     waterfalls.
   * Acclimatization Day at a Mid-Altitude Lodge: Before starting the main ascent, include a day and
     night at a lodge around Nanyuki or the lower slopes of Mt. Kenya (e.g., Ol Pejeta Conservancy).
     This helps the body adjust to the altitude and reduces the risk of altitude sickness.
   * Trout Fishing on Lake Ellis or Lake Michaelson: For clients on a longer trek (especially on the
     Chogoria route), a half-day can be dedicated to trout fishing in the crystal-clear alpine lakes.
     This requires park permits and gear.
   * Guided Forest Walk and Bird Watching: On the first or last day of the trek, offer a guided walk
     through the lower montane forest with a naturalist guide to identify the unique bird species and
     learn about the different vegetation zones.
   * Technical Climbing Instruction: For very adventurous clients, you could partner with a certified
     mountaineering guide to offer a basic rock climbing and rappelling session on some of the
     smaller rock faces near one of the high-altitude camps (like Shipton's Camp).
