## Competitor price reality check (so your UI ranges feel believable)

A few Kenya examples (all **per person** ranges, like your checklist requires ):

* **Masai Mara (3 days/2 nights)**: ~$640–$1,435 pp depending season + lodge + private vs shared. ([Masai Mara Travel][1])
* **Amboseli (2 days/1 night)**: ~$513–$1,098 pp depending accommodation tier (budget → luxury). ([Monkey Adventures][2])
* **Lake Nakuru + Naivasha (2 days/1 night)**: ~$515–$947 pp depending lodge + vehicle. ([Monkey Adventures][3])
* **Samburu (3 days/2 nights)**: ~$590–$1,250 pp.
* **Mt Longonot + Naivasha day trip**: local operators list **KES 10,000** for citizens, and ~$175–$250 pp for non-residents depending group size. ([Perfect Wilderness][4])
* **Diani packages** (domestic-style deals): operators advertise ~KES 17,600 up to ~KES 47,800+ depending hotel/tier. ([Bonfire Adventures][5])
* **Ol Pejeta day trip**: you’ll see anything from ~$180 pp up to ~$535 pp depending operator + group size inclusions. ([Kifaru Discover Safaris][6])
* **Official fees can change** (important for tooltips): KWS published updated conservation fees effective Oct 1, 2025. ([Kenya Wildlife Service][7])

**Takeaway for your UI:** keep ranges **wide enough to be true**, then let the **Accommodation Tier selector** compress/expand what the user sees.

* “Add” button: **“Add to my wishlist”** (instead of “Add to cart”)
* Trip bag label: **“My Trip Bag”** or **“My Wishlist”**
* Must-have toggle:

  * ⭐ **Must-do**
  * 🤍 **If time**
* Pricing tooltip title: **“What this estimate covers”**
* Pricing disclaimer (short): **“Per person. Final quote depends on dates + availability.”**

## The one thing I’d change in your UI copy (tiny but huge for trust)

Everywhere you show money, include the pattern:

**“From $X–$Y /person” + (ⓘ tooltip: “Final quote depends on dates, group size, and availability.”)** 

It prevents disputes *and* makes your “concierge handoff” feel premium instead of vague.
